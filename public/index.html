HelixIQ's AI cross-references your SNP variants against peer-reviewed genomic research to surface the insights most relevant to your health, performance, and longevity.

FAQ

Common questions

Which DNA testing kits are compatible?+
HelixIQ works with raw data files from 23andMe and AncestryDNA. After completing your DNA test, simply download your raw data file from your account settings — it's a .txt file — and upload it to us after purchase.
How do you protect my genetic data?+
Your raw DNA file is used solely to generate your report and is permanently deleted immediately after processing. We never store, sell, or share your genetic data with any third parties. Your privacy is non-negotiable.
How quickly will I receive my report?+
Most reports are generated and delivered to your inbox within 2–5 minutes of uploading your file. In rare cases of high demand, delivery may take up to 15 minutes.
Is this a medical diagnosis?+
No. HelixIQ reports are for educational and informational purposes only. They are not a substitute for professional medical advice, diagnosis, or treatment. We strongly recommend consulting a qualified healthcare professional before making any health-related decisions based on your report.
What format is the report delivered in?+
Your report is delivered as a professionally formatted PDF to the email address you provide at checkout. The Full Genome Report typically runs 40–60 pages; individual reports are 15–25 pages each.
Can I purchase multiple reports separately?+
Yes. Each report is sold individually. However, the Full Genome Report ($149) provides better value than purchasing all three individual reports ($177 combined) and includes additional analysis not available elsewhere.

⚠ HelixIQ reports are generated for educational and informational purposes only. They are not intended to diagnose, treat, cure, or prevent any disease or medical condition, and do not constitute medical advice. Always consult a qualified healthcare professional before making any health-related decisions. Genetic predispositions do not determine health outcomes.
